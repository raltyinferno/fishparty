Gas Conditioning

After the water passes through the water treatment filters, it is divided into a hot and a cold loop for distribution to each rearing module. Water sent to the incubation and start up rearing tanks has to been conditioned to remove high concentrations of carbon dioxide and dissolved nitrogen. Carbon dioxide is removed by passing water through a gas conditioning tower which removes the carbon dioxide and adds oxygen to the water. From there the water flows into vacuum degassing towers which apply vacuum pressure to the water removing the dissolved nitrogen.
